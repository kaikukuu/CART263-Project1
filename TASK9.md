The project that I have for review of the final projects is called Click the Cube whch is a continuation of their first cookie clicker inspired project I. In their explanation video Jason and Carter discussed their process for the second iteration on their game. They demonstrated a lot of the changes from the previous version which I think have made a lot of improvements. This version drew me in a lot more than previously by taking a simple concept and building more varying visuals that engage players to want to continue to see the next levels of clicking. Their project seems to be well adapted to the requirements of using new javascript libraries and they used mulitple libraries on top of three.js. I found that using these libraries really made for pretty cool visuals as well as add interesting effects for the players to mess around with. It gives a lot more liveliness to the playthrough and more variabilities for each run. I liked the particle effect changes that were demonstrated the longer the player is "breaking the system" through having more randomness, making substantial visual changes as to how the player interaction feels each next click on the now spinning cube (instead of a 2d button). I also found the effects of breaking the cube as the player breaks the system to create a strong immersion of the other visual implementations. 

An idea that came up if they wanted to continue to expand on this project's concept could be to create separate themed ones that each embody a theme like: foods, animals, locations ect... This could he;p players to be more invested upon playing in a visual theme that they chose and make them want to keep clicking to see what happens next. I do also commend them for creating and implementing a mobile version of them game that introduces a touch gesture instead of the click mechanic. It shows interest in adapting to the different audiences nowadays as many people only have a touch-screen device or would rather not use a computer or laptop and the popularity of mobile gaming around the world.
